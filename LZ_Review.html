The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild

The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild is an action-adventure game developed and published by Nintendo for the Nintendo Switch and Wii U consoles. Released in March 2017, it is part of the long-running The Legend of Zelda series. The game is set in a vast open world where players control Link, who awakens from a long slumber to defeat Calamity Ganon and save the kingdom of Hyrule. Breath of the Wild is praised for its innovative gameplay mechanics, including a physics-based engine that allows for creative problem-solving, exploration, and combat. The game features a non-linear structure, allowing players to explore the world freely and tackle challenges in any order they choose. It has received widespread critical acclaim for its design, art style, and immersive experience, making it one of the most celebrated entries in the series.

The Good

  • True Open World Freedom: This is the most important part. From the minute you start, you can go anywhere you see. If you can see a mountain, you can climb it. The game encourages you to leave the path and discover things on your own. It changed how open-world games are made.
  • Exploration is Always Rewarding: The world is massive, but it never feels empty. Every corner hides a puzzle (Shrine), a secret Korok Seed, or a valuable resource. You are constantly motivated to explore because you know you will find something useful or surprising.
  • Physics and Tools are Amazing: The game gives you cool tools (like Magnesis and Stasis) that let you solve puzzles in many creative ways. The physics system is incredibly detailed, allowing for fun experiments, especially during combat and in the Shrines.

The Bad

  • Dungeons Are Too Similar: While the Shrines are excellent, the main Divine Beast dungeons can start to feel a bit too much alike. They lack the unique thematic variety and big boss fights of older Zelda games.
